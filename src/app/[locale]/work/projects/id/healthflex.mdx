## Overview

HealthFlex is an orthopaedic wellness app that helps people manage musculoskeletal health, reduce pain, and recover from surgery with AI-driven motion tracking instead of guesswork. Rather than relying on infrequent in-clinic check-ins, patients get continuous, sensor-based feedback on their movement — reviewed and guided by orthopaedic professionals.

## Key Features

- **AI-Powered Motion Tracking**: Motion sensors capture movement data during exercises, which the app processes to measure form, range of motion, and progress over time.
- **Doctor-Supervised Recovery Plans**: Every wellness plan — exercises, activity modifications, lifestyle guidance — is set and monitored by orthopaedic specialists and physiotherapists, not just an algorithm.
- **Progress Tracking**: Patients and clinicians can see recovery trends session over session, replacing subjective self-reporting with objective data.
- **Personalized Wellness Plans**: Programs adapt to the individual's condition, whether it's post-surgical rehab, chronic pain management, or performance-focused recovery.
- **Remote Session Support**: Structured at-home sessions extend clinical care beyond the clinic, keeping recovery consistent between in-person visits.

## Technologies Used

- **React Native**: Cross-platform mobile app for iOS and Android from a single codebase.
- **Bluetooth**: Connects the app to wearable motion sensors, streaming movement data in real time during exercises.

## Outcome

HealthFlex is available on the [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/in/app/healthflex/id6478862632) and [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=in.healthflex.app), and now underpins the at-home technology layer of **Stance Health**, a Bengaluru-based musculoskeletal care startup founded by the same team (Rohit Arora and Ninad Karandikar). Stance Health combines sports orthopaedics, physiotherapy, and strength & conditioning into a single recovery model, using HealthFlex for real-time movement feedback between in-clinic sessions. Stance Health raised **$1 million in pre-seed funding** led by General Catalyst, with participation from Antler, DeVC, EX Capital, and angel investors including Swiggy co-founders Sriharsha Majety and Nandan Reddy — backing that reflects growing confidence in tech-driven musculoskeletal care in India.
